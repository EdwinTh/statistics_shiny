Count data is probably self-evident from its name. Variables start at zero and are increased every time some event occurs. The number of daily customer complaints are an example, or the number of times our website gets mentioned in the media. The underlying distribution has a rate, which is basically the mean of the counts over multiple observations. In order to estimate it you have to enter the mean of the observed counts and the number of observations. For the example of daily customer complaints you calculate the average number of complaints and the number of days you took measures.
